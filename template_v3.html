Who I am

"The customer doesn't care about features — they care about solving their problems."

I'm a Solutions Architect based in Dallas with a CS degree from UNT and an AWS Solutions Architect – Associate certification. At Inspirus, an HR tech SaaS platform, my job is to make enterprise clients see exactly how our technology solves their problem and why it's the right fit, before they've signed anything. Over the years, doing countless demos and learning from the best in my organization, one thing became clear: it's never about showing off the flashiest features. It's about finding the right solution fit for the client's core problem. That means running deep discovery with HR leaders and executives, architecting the solution around what they actually need and building demos that speak their language and not ours. I write the SOWs, run the PoCs and work on implementation directly with the client from start to finish.

I also serve in the US Army Reserves. It's about as different from tech and sales as it sounds. But joining the Army was the best decision of my life. During COVID I went through a low point and I wanted to prove myself and push myself. The Army gave me that and more. It taught me how to be a stronger leader, how to have a people-first mindset — because you're only as good as your weakest team member and growing together is the only way forward. It taught me to believe in myself more. It also shaped how I operate under pressure: high-stakes decisions, no excuses, execute.

Outside of work, I make lofi music and shoot street photography. They both teach me to slow down sometimes in life to notice and appreciate the smallest details in everyday life. A crumbling wall, a stranger mid-stride, a quiet street at the wrong hour.
